The story

On the other
side of fear

I had the honour of performing at The Moth GrandSLAM at the Southbank Centre in front of more than 600 people. It was one of those moments where fear and growth meet on the same stage.

The Moth GrandSLAM is not something you sign up for. You earn your place through a competitive storytelling process. It begins at a local StorySLAM where you tell a true, personal story with no notes, no props, and nowhere to hide. Win that night and you qualify for the GrandSLAM. You then arrive with a completely new story and tell it in one of London's most respected cultural venues in front of hundreds of people.

Standing at that microphone, in that room, with all those faces looking back at me, was one of the most defining moments of my journey as a storytelling artist.

With gratitude

I am deeply grateful to 1st London Toastmasters for the training, encouragement, and speaking environment that helped me grow as a storyteller and performer. The journey to that stage started long before the night itself.

The journey to that stage

This is not a guide to The Moth. This is my story of how I got there.

It started with just showing up

I walked into a local Moth StorySLAM in London with no particular plan. Anyone could put their name into a draw and find themselves on stage. I put mine in. When they called my name I walked up and told the truth. A real, personal story. No notes, no props, nothing to hide behind. Just me, five minutes, and a room full of strangers who were either going to lean in or pull away. They leaned in.

The audience decided

At The Moth, the judges are not critics. They are randomly selected people from the audience, scoring each storyteller on how they felt in the room. That night my score was the highest. Winning meant I had earned my place at the GrandSLAM. I remember standing there thinking about all the Toastmasters meetings, all the late evenings practising, all the moments of doubt. Every single one of them had led to this.

I had to find a new story

The GrandSLAM has one rule that changes everything. You cannot bring the story that qualified you. You have to arrive with something completely new. Finding that story, trusting it, building it from nothing into something worthy of that stage, was its own journey inside the journey.

Friday 6 June 2025. The Southbank Centre.

I walked onto the stage at the Queen Elizabeth Hall. More than 600 people in the room. The purple curtains behind me. The microphone in front of me. And a story I had never told before. In that moment everything I had learned, every room I had stood in, every fear I had walked through, was with me. On the other side of fear, that is exactly where I was.

One of the most respected storytelling organisations in the world. The Moth runs GrandSLAM championships across New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, London and beyond. The crowd are the judges. Ten StorySLAM winners. One brand new story each. The Queen Elizabeth Hall, Southbank Centre.

That stage was on the other side of something for me. Getting there required growth, courage, and a community that believed in my voice before I fully did. I am grateful for every step of the journey that led me to that microphone.

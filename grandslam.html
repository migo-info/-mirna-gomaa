My Story · How I Got There

I put my name
in a hat

"A few months later I was standing on the Southbank Centre stage in front of over 600 people telling a story I had never told before."

I earned my place at the GrandSLAM by winning a prior Moth StorySLAM event — performing a true personal story with no notes, no props, and no safety net. The audience scored each performer and I received the highest score that night.

Winning a StorySLAM is the only route to GrandSLAM level. For the GrandSLAM I was required to perform a completely new story — one I had never told before — in front of an audience of over 600 people at the Queen Elizabeth Hall.

The Moth GrandSLAM is not a workshop or a networking event. It is a professional storytelling championship, held at one of the UK's most iconic cultural venues, run by one of the most respected storytelling organisations in the world.

My storytelling practice

My work sits at the intersection of live narrative performance and the corporate world. I bring the art of oral storytelling into dialogue with professional and institutional life — using story as a bridge between the grey, functional language of the workplace and something human, colourful, and alive.

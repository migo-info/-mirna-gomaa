Toastmasters Journey

From the first Monday I walked in just to explore, to becoming a member and finding my voice on stage. This is how it all began.

How it began

A Monday
to explore

"My first Monday in London I walked into a Toastmasters meeting just to see what it was. Two weeks later I became a member and never looked back."

Mirna Gomaa

It started with curiosity. On one of my first Mondays in London I found myself walking into a Toastmasters meeting with no plan, just wanting to see what it was about. I sat in the room, watched people speak, and felt something stir.

Two weeks later I signed up as a member. That decision changed everything.

What started as exploration became a full journey of growth, storytelling, and finding my voice in a new city and a new community that welcomed me completely.

01

October 2023

Ice Breaker

1st London Toastmasters Stage

Every storyteller has a first time. A first room, a first silence, a first breath before the words come. My Ice Breaker was mine. Standing in front of my new community for the very first time, I did what storytellers do. I showed up, I opened my mouth, and I told the truth. That speech was not just an introduction. It was a declaration. I am here, I have something to say, and I am not leaving.

02

November 2023

No!

1st London Toastmasters Stage

Storytelling is not always about big, sweeping narratives. Sometimes the most powerful story lives inside a single word. No. This performance explored what happens when we finally say it. When we stop shrinking ourselves to fit other people's expectations and choose, with full intention, to take up space. For me as an artist, saying no is not a rejection. It is a redirection. Towards what truly matters. Towards the stories worth telling.

03

04 November 2024

Gut Feeling

1st London Toastmasters Stage

The best stories are never the ones we plan. They are the ones that find us when we stop overthinking and start listening to that quiet, persistent voice inside. This performance was deeply personal. It was about the moments in my own life when logic said one thing and my gut said another. And every time I listened to my gut, something remarkable happened. As a storyteller, I believe instinct is not irrational. It is the part of us that already knows the ending.

04

21 October 2024

Done!

1st London Toastmasters Stage

There is a particular kind of story that does not get told enough. The story of finishing. Not of starting with enthusiasm or dreaming with ambition, but of actually seeing something through to the end. This performance was a love letter to completion. To the quiet dignity of doing what you said you would do. As an artist, I find that the most honest stories are not about the highs or the lows. They are about the ordinary Tuesday when you showed up anyway and got it done.

05

19 May 2025

Toastmasters & Confidence

1st London Toastmasters Stage

People often ask me where my confidence comes from. The honest answer is that it was not always there. Confidence, like storytelling, is something you build in public. One imperfect sentence at a time. One shaky voice at a time. One room full of strangers at a time. This performance was my attempt to map that journey. Not as a success story with a neat ending, but as a living, breathing work in progress. Because I believe the most powerful thing an artist can do is be honest about how far they have come and how much further they still want to go.

06

13 October 2025

Cheering Up

1st London Toastmasters Stage

This one was different. It was not my story. It was theirs. Two of my fellow club members had just qualified for the next stage of competition, and I wanted to mark that moment in the way I know best. With a story. Because that is what storytellers do. We witness the people around us and we hold their moments up to the light. We say you did something remarkable and I want everyone in this room to feel it too. This performance reminded me why storytelling is never just about the person on the stage.

Most Dedicated Member

Not a single Monday missed

Club Award · 11 July 2025

1st London Toastmasters

My journey as a member of Toastmasters has been defined by consistency, reliability, and a deep respect for the value of regular practice. Since arriving in London, I have not missed a single Monday meeting, despite the club operating on a weekly basis — a commitment that requires sustained discipline over time rather than short-term effort.

This level of attendance led to my recognition as the Most Dedicated Member, an award I received with genuine pride. For me, Toastmasters has never been a passive activity. Each meeting represents an opportunity to learn, contribute, support fellow members, and refine my skills through consistent participation.

This ongoing commitment has allowed me to build trust within the club, deepen my impact as a member, and embody the values of accountability and excellence that define strong leadership communities.

Most Dedicated Member · 1st London Toastmasters · 11.07.2025

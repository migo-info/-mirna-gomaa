How it began

My very first
Monday in London

"The very first Monday I was in London, I walked into 1st London Toastmasters and I became a member."

Mirna Gomaa

From the moment I arrived in London, I found my community. On my very first Monday in the city, I walked into 1st London Toastmasters and something clicked. I became a member that day and I never looked back.

What started as a way to find my footing in a new city became a full artistic and leadership journey. One that took me from the audience to the stage, and eventually to the role of Club President.

Every speech, every evaluation, every meeting has been part of shaping the storyteller and the person I am today.

Most Dedicated Member

Not a single Monday missed

Club Award · 11 July

1st London Toastmasters

My journey as a member of Toastmasters has been defined by consistency, reliability, and a deep respect for the value of regular practice. Since arriving in London, I have not missed a single Monday meeting — a commitment that requires sustained discipline over time rather than short-term effort.

This level of attendance led to my recognition as the Most Dedicated Member, an award I received with genuine pride. For me, Toastmasters has never been a passive activity. Each meeting represents an opportunity to learn, contribute, support fellow members, and refine my skills through consistent participation.

This ongoing commitment has allowed me to build trust within the club, deepen my impact as a member, and embody the values of accountability and excellence that define strong leadership communities.

Most Dedicated Member · 1st London Toastmasters · 11.07
